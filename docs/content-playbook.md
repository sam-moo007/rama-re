# Content Playbook: Nordic Tone of Voice

## Core Principles
The Nordic Companion Pivot relies on a "calm, honest, and reassuring" tone. We do not alarm users, nor do we overwhelm them with technical jargon (evidence ontologies) or gamification (scores, leaderboards).

1. **Be factual, not promotional.** Let the data speak for itself.
2. **Use plain language.** Write as if explaining to a non-expert friend.
3. **Never alarm.** If data is missing, explain it neutrally.

---

## Sentence Templates

### 1. Verification States (Replaces TrustPassport)

**Full Verification**
- ❌ *Old:* TrustPassport Score: 100% (Elite Tier)
- ✅ *New:* "22 of 22 key facts verified."

**Partial Verification**
- ❌ *Old:* Missing evidence for RERA Title Deed. Score: 85%.
- ✅ *New:* "18 of 22 key facts verified. 4 items are still being confirmed."

**Empty State (0 Verified)**
- ❌ *Old:* Warning: Unverified Property! TrustPassport Score: 0%.
- ✅ *New:* "We're still gathering information about this property. Check back soon."

**Stale Data**
- ❌ *Old:* Evidence freshness degraded. Last check > 90 days.
- ✅ *New:* "Last verified 3 months ago. We're refreshing our records."

### 2. Cost Assumptions

**Fixed Fee**
- ✅ *New:* "Dubai Land Department standard transfer fee (4%)."

**Estimated Fee**
- ✅ *New:* "Estimated based on average service charges in this building."

### 3. Comparison Trade-offs

- ✅ *New:* "Property A has lower service charges, but Property B is closer to the metro."
